10 BIGGEST RIVERS ON EARTH

Is the longest river in the world the Amazon or the Nile? We answer and provide a list and characteristics of the largest rivers in the world. Speaking about the length of rivers, scientists usually give different numbers, because it all depends on what is considered the source of the river. "My Planet" has compiled a list of the ten longest rivers on the planet. But places in the ranking can be distributed in other ways.

THE LARGEST RIVER IN THE WORLD. AMAZON: 6800 KM

For a long time, the Nile was considered the largest river, but in 2007, Brazilian scientists discovered a new starting point for the Amazon - a stream flowing from a glacier on Mount Mismi. The area of ​​the Amazon Basin is 6.9 million km2, which is about twice the size of India. It flows through Brazil, Peru, Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador, Venezuela, Guyana. And its length can be compared with the distance between St. Petersburg and Chita by car. In addition, the Amazon is the deepest river in the world. The average volume of water discharged into the Atlantic Ocean is 219,000 m3/s.
